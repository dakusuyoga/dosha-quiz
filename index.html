READ YOUR FULL TYPE

The classical texts describe your constitution — your Prakṛti — in terms of three energies, or Doṣa-s: Vāta (V), Pitta (P), and Kapha (K). From these arise the constitutional types: three single-Doṣa types, the dual types, the tri-Doṣic VPK, and the near-perfectly Balanced.

Most people are a blend. Pure single-Doṣa types are less common, and the two rarest types sit at opposite ends of the spectrum: the tri-Doṣic VPK, who carries all three energies in strength, and the Balanced individual, in whom the three sit in quiet equilibrium.

A word on the dual types. When two Doṣa-s share the lead, the order matters: a constitution can lean in either direction depending on which Doṣa predominates. Vāta-Pitta and Pitta-Vāta are built from the same two energies, but the one named first sets the tone.

Vāta (V)

Your nature is movement, air, and change.

You are likely thin, and you gain weight only with difficulty — except when you overeat with real determination, which you may do to steady yourself or to fuel the next burst of activity. Your frame tends to be narrow through the shoulders and hips, and your joints often crack when you move. You fidget; like a tendency toward heaviness in others, fidgeting tends to run in families.

Above all, you are dry. Your skin chaps easily and is prone to corns and calluses; your hair tends to be coarse, dry, and curly. You struggle with cold and often complain of poor circulation in your hands and feet. Your skin is usually cool to the touch, you sweat little, and you love to be out in the sun.

Your appetite is irregular, and your love of excitement pulls you into irregular eating that only worsens your digestion. Chronic constipation is a familiar companion, a product of your innate astringency. You love soupy, oily, hot foods, but you tend to go to extremes — either indulging in heavy, hard-to-digest dishes or denying yourself all richness at once.

Your energy comes in spurts and bursts rather than a steady stream. Too often you prop it up with pungent stimulants like coffee rather than admit you are tired. You can sustain a pitch of frenzied activity for a while, but exhaustion always follows, and you may not notice it until sheer fatigue forces you to stop. Sleep can be elusive: you either struggle to fall or stay asleep, or you stay so exhausted that when you finally rest you sleep like the dead.

You feel pain more keenly than other types, and loud noise wears on you — your nervous system seems to carry less insulation than it needs. That deep drive to avoid pain can surface as fear. This is why you adore oil massage: it soothes and quiets an overactive nervous system, easing both physical and mental tenderness. You live an erratic life because routine is genuinely hard for you to build, and your restless, bitter-tinged curiosity keeps you forever tinkering with yourself and your surroundings. If changeability defines most of what you do, you are Vāta-predominant.

Pitta (P)

Your nature is fire, heat, and sharp intensity.

You run hot — intense, driven, and quick to irritation. You are usually medium in height, weight, and endurance. Your skin tends to be light and reddens fast in the sun, after exercise, or when you blush; you sunburn easily and often carry plenty of freckles and moles. Your hair is typically straight and light in colour. Anyone whose hair is naturally red is at least part Pitta.

You sweat easily, because of all that heat stored inside. The sour and salty in your nature keep your appetite reliably strong, and you love to eat — food and drink take the edge off the fire. Miss a meal and you may "consume" some unlucky bystander with your stored anger instead. You enjoy nearly all foods and usually digest them well; you tend toward loose stools and are rarely constipated.

That same fire sharpens your mind. You think quickly and grow impatient around people who are slower or less focused than you. You usually sleep well, because you regard sleep as sensible — but let yourself become obsessed with work and you'll spend the nights awake. You bring the same intensity and competitiveness to everything, work or play, and your pungent edge means you anger easily, even when you don't outwardly lose your temper.

Kapha (K)

Your nature is earth, water, and steady strength.

You are likely a solid, heavyset build — a natural athlete when you exercise properly, and someone who seems to gain weight just by looking at food when you neglect it. You are healthy most of the time, especially when you don't overeat. You don't feel the fierce physical hunger that Vāta and Pitta types do, because sweetness runs strong in you by nature — but you can grow attached to food as a form of emotional comfort. You sleep soundly and tend to oversleep.

You generally don't crave the excitement and stimulation that Vāta and Pitta chase, even where sex is concerned — though once you are drawn in, the sour and salty rise in you and your appetites wake up. You are stable, unhurried, and inclined to be content. That attachment to a comfortable status quo is your gift and your trap: it can make you averse to change, and at its worst it hardens into greed, stubbornness, or resistance to anything new. Where Vāta needs grounding and Pitta needs a challenge, you need motivation and stimulation to keep from settling too deep.

Vāta-Pitta (VP)

You carry the lightness of air and the intensity of fire.

You generally share Vāta's poor circulation and love of heat — but your Pitta side sets firm limits on how much heat you can actually endure. The Pitta in you loves to eat; the Vāta in you makes large meals hard to digest. Many of your traits are visible blends of the two: wavy hair, for instance, is Vāta's curl meeting Pitta's straightness.

Often, though, the two don't blend so much as take turns. When you are out of balance, fear alternates with anger as your response to stress, and that swing can tip into bullying or domineering behaviour. The Pitta in you feels the need to command; the Vāta in you plants doubt about your fitness to command — and the uneasy compromise can become the domination of those weaker than yourself.

At your best, you marry Vāta's capacity for original thought to Pitta's skill at putting theory to work. Lightness and intensity are the qualities V and P share, and your work is to harness that lightness for intensive self-development. Left unguided, it runs the other way: Vāta's pull toward numbing pain and Pitta's pull toward amplifying intensity can drag you into deeper patterns of addiction than either type knows alone.

Pitta-Vāta (PV)

Fire leads in you, with the wind close behind.

You share the same two energies as the Vāta-Pitta type, but the order is reversed: here Pitta sets the tone. You lead with heat, precision, and the drive to transform things, and Vāta's movement and creativity ride on top of that fire. Among the dual constitutions you are often the most dynamic and energetic — quick-minded, ambitious, and inventive — though you also overstimulate more easily than most, because the reactive nervous system of Vāta is wired to an engine that already runs hot.

Physically you tend toward a medium or lean build, lighter than a pure Pitta, with muscle that is reasonably developed rather than heavy. Your body runs warm. Your skin is sensitive, warm, and on the thinner side, and it may alternate between oily and dry as the two Doṣa-s trade places. Your hair is often fine and may grey or thin earlier than most, and your eyes tend to be bright and penetrating, frequently sensitive to light. In temperature you lean cool — you dislike excessive heat, yet you can carry cold hands and feet (Vāta) over a core that still feels hot inside (Pitta) at the very same time.

Yours is one of the most variable digestions of any constitution. Your appetite is strong and you grow irritable when meals are delayed, but your digestion fluctuates under stress, tipping toward acidity, bloating, and gas, and swinging between constipation and loose stools. Mentally you are formidable: intelligent, analytical, a fast learner, imaginative and articulate, an efficient and innovative problem-solver. That same wiring brings impatience, anxiety, perfectionism, overthinking, and a real difficulty switching off — your mind stays lit long after you'd like it to dim. Sleep is often light and easily interrupted, rich with vivid dreams, and hard to enter at all when your thoughts are still racing. You thrive on vigorous, challenging movement and on learning new skills, but overtraining, relentless competition, and chronic short sleep will cost you. When you fall out of balance, it tends to show as acid reflux, gastritis, headaches or migraines, insomnia, anxiety, burnout, inflammatory skin, and bowels that alternate between bound and loose.

Pitta-Kapha (PK)

You combine the fire of Pitta with the ground of Kapha.

You probably adjust to today's confusion, irregularity, and constant change better than any other constitution, because you pair Kapha's stability with Pitta's adaptability. Many people who achieve all-round success in life are PKs. Pitta's active metabolism balances Kapha's powerful physique to keep you physically healthy, and Pitta's anger is well tempered by Kapha's caution to keep you mentally steady. You prefer temperate climates but can easily handle extremes of heat or cold, and you enjoy — and benefit from — vigorous exercise, sex included.

Your shadow rises from the oiliness and wetness that Pitta and Kapha share. The ease with which you succeed can feed Pitta's arrogance and overconfidence and Kapha's smug self-satisfaction, and together these can seal you off — completely and efficiently — from any reality other than the one you wish to see. This is where the oiliness shows: like water off a duck's back, criticism slides away unregarded while only flattery is let in. It can make a very successful PK very difficult to live with.

Because Vāta is naturally minimized in you, you need what Vāta provides: the dryness of introspection or spiritual discipline, and the irregularity of unpredictable situations, to keep overconfidence in check.

Kapha-Pitta (KP)

Ground leads in you, with fire held steadily within it.

You share the same two energies as the Pitta-Kapha type, but here Kapha sets the tone. You lead with the stability, endurance, and nourishing solidity of earth and water, and Pitta's metabolic strength and intensity burn steadily inside that frame rather than running the show. This is often described as one of the strongest constitutions physically — you have the build to do real work and the fire to power it.

You tend toward a medium-to-large frame with good muscle that you gain easily; you also gain fat more readily than a pure Pitta would. Your skin is thick and smooth, your hair lustrous, your stamina good. Your digestion is one of your quieter gifts: consistently strong, with a regular appetite and an efficient metabolism — though when you overreach it, you overeat, feel sluggish after meals, and can tip toward acid reflux and steady weight gain. You tolerate cold well, dislike humid heat, and generally feel warm overall. In mind you are practical, organized, methodical, and dependable, with an excellent memory and deep reserves of patience and determination.

Emotionally you are steady in a way lighter types envy — loyal, compassionate, disciplined, and resilient, hard to knock off course. Your shadow is the flip side of that solidity: stubbornness, possessiveness, competitiveness, and a difficulty letting go. Your anger is not Pitta's quick flare but something slower — it builds beneath the surface and, once roused, burns long and hot. You sleep deeply and long, and you are difficult to wake. You have excellent tolerance for resistance training, endurance work, and sustained effort, and you genuinely need enough intensity in your life to keep Kapha from settling into stagnation. When you fall out of balance, it tends to show as weight gain, high cholesterol, inflammatory skin conditions or acne, a sluggish metabolism, congestion, and — on the emotional side — suppressed anger and an over-tight grip on the people and things you love.

Vāta-Kapha (VK)

You are cold twice over — the air of Vāta meeting the water of Kapha.

Vāta and Kapha are united in coldness. You don't suffer physical cold as sharply as a pure Vāta, because Kapha lends you strength and insulation — but you carry a double emotional need for warmth. You tend to be tall, yet average in build and in most other physical qualities, much as Pitta types are. Your lack of heat usually shows up as digestive trouble, especially constipation, and respiratory conditions with heavy mucus are common too.

You are usually zealous about whatever you take on, and you often overdo it by forgetting to use discretion. You can be light, open, and airy by turns, then deep and secretive. Without a strong Pitta fire to integrate them, your two natures — diametrically opposed — are especially hard to reconcile, so you must be particularly wary of jumping to conclusions before you've properly investigated. And because Kapha's deep emotional nature is wedded to Vāta's up-and-down sensitivity, emotional hurt goes deep in you and stays deep.

Kapha-Vāta (KV)

Ground leads in you, with the wind moving quietly underneath.

You share the same two energies as the Vāta-Kapha type, but here Kapha sets the tone. Kapha gives you stability and structure; Vāta brings movement and variability beneath it. The result is a particular kind of person — calm and settled on the surface, while a good deal of mental activity runs on inside. To others you can look unhurried and unbothered; within, the wind is rarely still.

You tend toward a medium-to-large frame with a body weight that varies, and you're prone to water retention. One of your quiet paradoxes is dry skin over a heavier build — Vāta's dryness inside Kapha's fullness. Your joints tend to be thick, your hands and feet cold, your movements on the slower side. Yours is usually the weakest digestion of the dual constitutions: your appetite is irregular, your digestion slow, and you're prone to bloating, gas, constipation, and a heaviness that lingers after eating. You dislike cold weather, prefer warmth, and chill easily. In mind you are thoughtful, creative, patient, and reflective — reliable and compassionate, with an excellent long-term memory.

Emotionally you are nurturing, calm, loyal, and forgiving. Your challenges gather on the quieter end of the spectrum: worry, attachment, indecision, procrastination, a tendency to withdraw, and — when you're severely out of balance — a low, heavy mood that can be hard to shift, a fear of change married to a resistance to it. You sleep long, yet may still wake unrefreshed, and you lean toward oversleeping; when Vāta becomes aggravated, your dreams turn vivid. You do best with regular, warming movement and value consistency over intensity — too little activity lets Kapha stagnate, while too much irregular activity stirs Vāta up. When you fall out of balance, it tends to show as chronic constipation, edema, sinus congestion and seasonal allergies, fatigue and low mood, anxiety, stiffness in the joints and muscles, poor circulation, and weight gain that arrives despite an unreliable appetite.

VPK — Tri-Doṣic (V-P-K)

You carry all three energies in full strength.

Yours is one of the two rarest results. All three Doṣa-s — the movement of Vāta, the fire of Pitta, and the ground of Kapha — are present in you in roughly equal force. In balance, this is a remarkable constitution: you can draw on Vāta's creativity, Pitta's drive, and Kapha's endurance in turn, and few types are as broadly capable or as physically resilient when well cared for.

The difficulty is that balance is harder to hold and harder to recover. Where a single-Doṣa type has one tendency to manage, you have three, and when you slip, all three can go out of balance at once — the restlessness, the heat, and the heaviness arriving together rather than one at a time. This is why tri-Doṣic people who let all three energies fall out of order are rarely healthy without effort, and why a genuinely disciplined life — regular routine, attentive diet, steady self-observation — matters more for you than for almost anyone else. The reward for that discipline is unusual: kept in balance, you have range and stamina that narrower constitutions simply don't.

Balanced

Your three energies sit in quiet equilibrium.

Yours is the other rare result — and, in a sense, the fortunate one. In you, Vāta, Pitta, and Kapha exist in near-equal, settled proportion. None dominates, none is starved, and the qualities that pull other people to extremes tend to cancel gently in you toward the middle. You are usually healthy, and you stay that way because it takes real, sustained stress before an imbalance can take hold at all.

This does not make you invulnerable or exempt from care — it means your margin is wider. Where another type tips into imbalance from a poor week of sleep or a change of season, you can usually absorb the disturbance and return to centre on your own. The risk hidden in that resilience is complacency: because imbalance comes so rarely, it can be well established before you notice it, and because no single Doṣa announces itself loudly in you, the early signals are subtle. Your work is less about correcting a constant tendency and more about protecting a good thing — keeping the routines, diet, and rhythms that hold your equilibrium in place.

The dual-Doṣa portraits draw on classical Āyurvedic principles as set down in texts such as the Charaka Saṃhitā, Suśruta Saṃhitā, and Aṣṭāṅga Hṛdaya, alongside contemporary reviews and research into validated Prakṛti assessment tools. It's worth being straight about the evidence: modern research supports the reality of constitutional variation and has produced increasingly reliable ways to assess it, but the detailed personality and clinical profiles of the dual constitutions remain rooted primarily in classical doctrine rather than in large, independent clinical trials. Read these descriptions as a time-tested framework for understanding your tendencies — not as clinically validated fact about every trait.

A Note Before You Go

Constitutional types are simple, but not simplistic. Every body and mind is unlike any other, and your type does not lock you into a stereotype — it gives you information about metabolic tendencies so deeply ingrained that they must be actively balanced if you are to stay in balance.

Your constitution does not change, but your perception of it may. Come back to this evaluation after some time and you may find your first reading was off — which is normal, because as we grow healthier our sense of reality grows clearer, and what once seemed impossible to grasp can suddenly become plain. Even if you've misjudged your type today, living for a while according to your current condition will be therapeutic.

Think of your result as a foundation. If the foundation is good, what you build on it will be good; if it's imperfect, no amount of building can quite save the structure above it. This is the start of the road — the beginning of a longer, richer understanding of yourself.
